About

Building open-source tools for bioelectronic medicine

I'm a biomedical engineer and researcher working at the intersection of neuroscience, medical devices, and open-source technology. I received my PhD in Biomedical Engineering from USC, where I developed implantable microsensors and neuromodulation systems.

Currently, I serve as Director of Technology Integration and Dissemination at CARSS (Center for Autonomic Nerve Recording and Stimulation Systems), part of USC's Alfred E. Mann Department of Biomedical Engineering. Our work focuses on making medical device research more accessible through open-source platforms like OpenNerve.

What I'm working on

My research spans MEMS fabrication, wireless biomedical sensors, electrochemical sensing, and implantable electronics.
